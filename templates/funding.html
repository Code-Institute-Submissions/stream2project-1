Underfunding of education in Oregon

From birth through the launch of their careers, Oregon students must have the education, tools, and supports necessary to prepare them to succeed in a global economy.

Oregon receives significantly less funding than most other US states. Funding for education in California is three times higher and funding in New York is four times higher. Oregon students are falling behind their counterparts in other states as they lack the essential provisions required for a modern 21st century education

Crippling underfunding is driving up class sizes and forcing schools to cut corners. A narrow curriculum and a culture of assessment is driving away teachers, creating a recruitment and retention crisis.
